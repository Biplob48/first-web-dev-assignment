Pillars of "Bangladesh 2.0"

Innovation

Tech startups fuel economic growth. ICT advancements, Digital Bangladesh initiative, and support for entrepreneurship drive innovation forward.

Sustainability

Solar power systems electrify rural areas, supporting off-grid communities. Ongoing research in wind and biomass energy promotes sustainability.

Infrastructure

Developing robust digital networks, improving transportation systems, and building Hi-Tech Parks to support economic growth and technological advancements.

Empowerment

Social enterprises and NGOs drive financial inclusion, women's empowerment, and access to healthcare, transforming communities through innovative solutions.

The Vision of Future

Bangladesh 2.0 aims to redefine the nation’s trajectory by embracing cutting-edge technology, promoting inclusive growth, and ensuring sustainability. With initiatives in renewable energy, digital transformation, and robust infrastructure, the country is set to become a global model of development.

Digital Transformation

Creating a nationwide digital ecosystem to enhance connectivity and access to services for all citizens.

Green Energy

Investments in solar and wind energy to ensure a sustainable and eco-friendly future.

Modern Infrastructure

Building state-of-the-art infrastructure to support economic growth and global competitiveness.

Dr. Muhammad Yunus, a Nobel Peace Prize laureate and pioneer of microcredit, has been serving as the Chief Adviser of the Interim Government of Bangladesh since August 8, 2024. His appointment came after widespread calls for his leadership following the overthrow of the previous government1. As Chief Adviser, Dr. Yunus has been committed to implementing significant reforms across various sectors. He has focused on labour reforms, aiming to align Bangladesh's labour laws with International Labour Organization (ILO) standards. Additionally, he has urged all stakeholders to accept and implement the reports of various reform commissions, including those related to judicial, public administration, electoral system, police administration, anti-corruption, and constitutional reforms.Dr. Yunus has also been actively engaging with global investors and leaders, inviting them to invest in Bangladesh and highlighting the country's improved business and investment climate. His vision for a "new Bangladesh" emphasizes unity, transparency and democratic renewal

Bangladesh Launches New Satellite

Date: 12/05/2018

Time: 2:14 AM(GMT+6)

The Bangabandhu Satellite-1 (Bangabandhu-1) is the first Bangladeshi geostationary communications and broadcasting satellite. It is named after the first president of Bangladesh, Bangabandhu Sheikh Mujibur Rahman. It was manufactured by Thales Alenia Space and launched on 12 May 2018 from Kennedy Space Center, USA.The satellite was the first payload launched by a SpaceX Falcon 9 Block 5 launch vehicle.With the launch, Bangladesh became the 57th country to independently operate a satellite in space.
